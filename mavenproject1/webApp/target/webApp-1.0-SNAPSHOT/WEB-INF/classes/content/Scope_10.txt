<p>You surely would have noticed by now, the words <code><a href="/wiki/Java_Programming/Keywords/public" title="Java Programming/Keywords/public"><font style="color:DodgerBlue4; text-decoration: none; font-weight: bold;">public</font></a></code>, <code><a href="/wiki/Java_Programming/Keywords/protected" title="Java Programming/Keywords/protected"><font style="color:DodgerBlue4; text-decoration: none; font-weight: bold;">protected</font></a></code> and <code><a href="/wiki/Java_Programming/Keywords/private" title="Java Programming/Keywords/private"><font style="color:DodgerBlue4; text-decoration: none; font-weight: bold;">private</font></a></code> at the beginning of class's method declarations used in this book. These keywords are called the <b>access modifiers</b> in the Java language syntax, and they define the scope of a given item.</p>
