<p>Java was not the first virtual-machine-based platform, though it is by far the most successful and well-known. Previous uses for virtual machine technology primarily involved <a href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/emulators" class="extiw" title="w:emulators">emulators</a> to aid development for not-yet-developed hardware or operating systems, but the JVM was designed to be implemented entirely in software, while making it easy to efficiently port an implementation to hardware of all kinds.</p>
