<p>Co-founder of Sun Microsystems, Bill Joy, envisioned a language combining the power of Mesa and C in a paper he wrote for the engineers at Sun named <i>Further</i>. Gathering ideas, Gosling began work on enhancing C++ and named it "C++ ++ --", a pun on the evolutionary structure of the language's name. The ++ and -- meant, <i>putting in</i> and <i>taking out stuff</i>. He soon abandoned the name and called it <b>Oak</b><sup id="cite_ref-CITEREFEarlyYearsSun1_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CITEREFEarlyYearsSun1-1">[1]</a></sup> after the tree that stood outside his office.</p>
